The user wants writing — both chat answers and stored documents — done **for the reader**, not compressed for the writer:

- **Developed prose, not telegraphic.** He finds terse fragment-bullets hard to follow; write full, connected sentences. This applies to the **documents themselves**, not only chat. (He explicitly corrected an earlier attempt to keep artifacts terse while only expanding chat: one-pagers and pages must be human prose too.)
- **Write for a named audience, in their language.** Decide who the document is for first, then use their vocabulary. For an expert audience, name the domain terms they already know; for a lay audience, explain the underlying thing plainly instead of citing jargon or proper names they won't recognize.
- **No empty slogans.** Cut clever lines that carry no concrete meaning (e.g. "you don't collect, you connect" — it explains nothing); say the actual thing the reader can act on.
- **Keep machine-navigation out of human content.** Internal IDs and codes are useless to a reader and break the flow — a human won't cross-reference a bare code. Collect them in a separate **"For AI / source map"** section, and make anything a human might follow a real clickable link.

**Why:** he reads the documents as a person and hands them to others; compression and bare codes cost comprehension.

**How to apply:** full sentences everywhere, named-audience framing, no slogans, IDs in a separate AI section. Pairs with [[feedback_directness]] (no praise, disagree directly).
