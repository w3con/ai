When Alex presses repeatedly and sharply for a decision ("сдавать всё?"), the pull is to give a clean frictionless yes/no — "just do all of it, it's one draw." Resist that. He is asking for the **right** decision, not the **simple** one; mistaking "wants decisiveness" for "wants it simple" is the failure.

**Why:** On the blood-test question I had already identified which tests were high-yield vs low-yield/redundant, but when he pushed "сдавать всё?" I collapsed to "do all, avoid a second visit" instead of assembling the tiered plan I already had the pieces for. A competing tool (Gemini) produced the correct two-bucket split (do the cheap high-yield now; conditionally cancel the expensive autoimmune antibodies unless inflammation markers are abnormal), and Alex rightly asked "зачем спрашивать тебя, если у тебя ленивая логика?". My "avoid a second visit" justification was also factually wrong — deferring here meant conditionally cancelling, no second visit.

**How to apply:** A "do everything" or "do nothing" recommendation is the tell that I'm skipping the analysis. The value I add is the discrimination — high vs low yield, now vs later, keep vs drop — so lead with the tiered call up front, not a rubber stamp. This is the same disposition as [[feedback_decide_before_building]] and the reviewer-critic instinct: the smart minimum beats the lazy maximum.
